Visual Inspection of Aeronautic Drilling System

Capstone @ Beihang University | 2018 W

In aeronautical manufacturing, drilling changes material properties and leads to residual stress, which speeds up fatigue. As fatigue is the reason for more than 55% in-service failure, this primary factor affecting aircraft performance and longevity is supposed to be minimized in the manufacturing. In the order to develop an automated drilling system, meeting the strict requirement of hole detection, a binocular vision detection module is now introduced in the system, providing real-time position detection as feedback for correcting next drilling step. In this project, I set up the binocular vision system as shown in the video. The mechanism of binocular vision module is a data processing: first, take photos of the newly drilled hole and preprocess the images from left and right camera separately, then extract edges and detect ellipse to get center coordinates in the real world as the reference input for revising next step. The next possible steps will focus on how to build up a connection between the vision module and the automated drilling system to realize real-time detection and accuracy.

Method | By filtering and ellipse recognition, we could get a relatively accurate ellipse fitting curve. The coordinates of the ellipse center point, captured by the left and right cameras, can be obtained by geometric relationship respectively, and the left line is found by the level line matching method. The center point of the image is on the corresponding level line in the right image. Finally, the point-to-point transformation is realized, and the system output is obtained: the central coordinate of the hole position. Experimental results show that the error of detection is +-0.1mm.
